Options Trading Competition

Investopedia Simulator · Multi-Strategy Portfolio

Executed a multi-strategy options trading portfolio deploying Long Calls, Long Puts, Protective Puts, Straddles, and Strangles across major equities and ETFs. Achieved a peak portfolio value of $1.13M (+13.4%) before drawdown from volatility mispricing in straddle and put positions.

Methodology

  • Protective Puts: AAPL, NKE, NVDA — hedged stock positions with put insurance
  • Long Calls: NKE (biggest winner +$9,200), PLTR, and more
  • Long Puts: SPY, QQQ — directional bearish bets on indices
  • Straddles: NFLX, WMT, GOOG — earnings volatility plays

  • Overestimated implied volatility around earnings and macro events
  • Protective Puts provided effective downside protection
  • Tariff uncertainty and flat markets eroded time value on premium-heavy positions

Financial Econometrics

Research Paper — U.S. Market Spillover Effects on Global Indices

VAR-BEKK GARCH · 10-Year Global Study

Investigated return and volatility spillovers from the U.S. stock market (S&P 500) to 5 major global indices — FTSE 100 (UK), DAX (Germany), Nikkei 225 (Japan), IBOVESPA (Brazil), and MASI (Morocco) — across developed and emerging markets over a 10-year period (2014–2024). Employed VAR-BEKK GARCH modeling with Lee-Strazicich structural break tests to capture regime shifts during COVID-19, Russia-Ukraine conflict, and the 2024 U.S. elections.

VAR ModelBEKK-GARCHStructural BreaksVolatility SpilloversMATLABBloomberg TerminalEconometrics
Time Span2014–2024 (10 yrs)
Global IndicesSPX, FTSE, DAX, NKY, IBOV, MASI
SPX→UKX Spillover0.0197 → 0.1741
Structural BreaksMarch & April 2020
Wald TestAll H₀ rejected (p<0.05)

Methodology

  • Lee-Strazicich LM tests identifying structural breaks around COVID-19 (March 2020)
  • VAR(1) model for return spillovers: pre-crisis vs post-crisis coefficient comparison
  • BEKK-GARCH(1,1) for volatility spillovers with Cholesky decomposition
  • Wald test for return, volatility, and joint spillover hypothesis testing
  • Data sourced from Bloomberg Terminal via BQL, cleaned and modeled in MATLAB

Key Findings

  • U.S. market confirmed as dominant transmitter of global financial shocks
  • Post-crisis SPX→UKX return spillover surged 8.8× (0.0197 → 0.1741)
  • Developed markets showed faster recovery; emerging markets lagged with structural fragility
  • BEKK off-diagonal GARCH coefficients increased from ~0 to 0.1328 post-crisis
  • Policy-driven recovery amplified market synchronization globally

Corporate Finance

Midland Energy Resources: Cost of Capital

HBS Case Study · WACC Analysis

Calculated divisional and corporate WACC for Midland Energy Resources ($248.5B revenue), a global energy company with E&P, Refining & Marketing, and Petrochemicals divisions. Derived unobservable divisional betas using asset-weighted decomposition and estimated a 9.86% WACC for the Petrochemicals division.

WACCCAPMBeta EstimationCost of DebtCapital StructureDivisional Analysis
Company Revenue$248.5B
Petrochem WACC9.86%
Petrochem Beta1.88
Cost of Debt5.89%
After-Tax CoD3.55%
Tax Rate (Avg)39.73%

Methodology

  • Cost of Debt from risk-free rate (1Y Treasury 4.54%) + credit spread (AA- → 1.35%)
  • Asset-weighted beta decomposition: derived Petrochemicals β = 1.88
  • CAPM cost of equity using 10Y Treasury (4.66%) + β × EMRP (5.0%)
  • Average 3-year effective tax rate calculation (39.73%)
  • WACC with target D/V = 40% and E/V = 60%

Key Findings

  • Petrochemicals carries higher systematic risk (β = 1.88) than E&P or R&M
  • Cost of equity at 14.06% reflects elevated market sensitivity
  • WACC of 9.86% serves as the hurdle rate for division capital allocation
  • Analysis informed by comparable company data from Exhibit 5 of the HBS case
